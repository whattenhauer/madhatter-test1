I'm Bill Hattenhauer.  In the past few years I've returned to the West Coast to further an ancient ambition of mine, to sail.  I became interested in sailing as a young teen, with the story of Robin Lee Graham's solo sail around the world. While stationed in Del Rio, TX, I was fortunate to know an owner of a sailboat dealership and was invited to sail with him in a race, I had a great time and we even won.   The dream slept till I was in New Mexico, while working there I met Earl , who owned a Moorings boat.   We would talk occasionally about the boat and about how he could afford it, with some questioning he revealed his joys and sorrows of ownership.  The dream of owning and sailing my own boat turned over and went to sleep again. 

 BUT when I got back to the coast one of the first things I did with my vacation time and money was to buy into a US Sail Basic Keel Boat Certification training course, and I scheduled it for the nastiest weather season of the year.  I figured if I didn't like it when it was cold, rough, and raining then I wasn't giving it a fair shot.  Well with Basic Cruising Certification behind me and Bare Boat Certification in front of me, I make it a tradition to pick nasty weather months to train in.  Frankly you get to do things and gain a level of confidence out training in foul weather that I didn't find in the fair weather training sessions. I'm still looking at owning a boat of my own and saving my nickels for that too. Some of the best times I've had with my nearly grown children have been sailing.  Club Nautique here in So. Cal. and in the San Francisco Bay area have a great set of instructors, and a fairly nice fleet.
