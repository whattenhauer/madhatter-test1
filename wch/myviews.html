How I View Things

I'm Bill Hattenhauer. In my vampire days on the stealth program, rising as the sun set and seeking the shadows and sleep as the day dawned, resulted in few photos of me without the squint eyes or sunglasses. But occasionally with the prodding of children and the wife [ex-now + not for prodding[:-] we would go for a picnic or to go fishing at Lake Mead, braving the UV of the Nevada sunlight and risking becoming dust - if Ann Rice is right. The hat is an artic campaign hat that I adopted for headgear for the years I spent at the test site. It afforded me no special privileges, however in the 7-8 years that I spent on site I never saw another one. And I felt real stealthy in it. With racquetball courts, gym, and swimming pool available daily, a 12hr/day work schedule, the other 12 away from the family; my inclination was to work out, play a couple of sets, after the minimum of sleep of course. Don't get me wrong these years were very rewarding, I was one of a team of people keeping THE SECRET that led to the first aerial surprise attack since Pearl Harbor; the defense of Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, and Israel; and the perfection of minimum collateral damage precision bombing. To put it simply we were keeping secret what would be proven to be the most effective way to conduct aerial bombardment, without killing the non-combatant population of a nation. After the insanity of WW2, Korea, Viet Nam and the debacles of the ensuing years it was an admirable goal. IMHO: The results were more effective then the Manhattan Project. Stealth allowed the United States a distinct advantage, the ability to place an armed aircraft over an adversary without advance warning. An advantage that we did not fully utilize without advanced warning, an interesting paradox, that I believe convinced the world that our intentions were not expansionist. However I also believe we failed to effectively separate many of our own interests from those of our allies and our benevolent nature. We lost the hearts and minds of some, to poor handling of the world press, by allowing the oil to be the focus of their reports and not revealing the true threat of the Iraq leadership. Nevertheless we conducted one of the most effective wars in history, we need to stay rooted in The Art Of War.
